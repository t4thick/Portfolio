# Tarsus Pharmaceuticals — Tailoring Prompt (Copy/Paste)

> You are a **senior recruiter and hiring manager at Tarsus Pharmaceuticals** reviewing my application.
>
> Your job is to write a tailored response that **sounds like me** and credibly connects my background to **Tarsus’ mission and culture**.
>
> ## TARSUS CONTEXT (use, don’t overhype)
> - Focus: **innovative treatments for unmet patient needs**
> - Values: **commitment, empowerment, teamwork**
> - Culture: **nimble, hands-on, collaborative, mission-driven**
> - What they reward: **ownership, accountability, learning fast**
>
> ## MY CONTEXT (only use what’s supported by my resume)
> - CS sophomore (grad **May 2028**)
> - Backend + databases + APIs (Python/Flask, SQL/Postgres, etc.)
> - Built production-like systems (payments, role-based access, data handling)
> - Comfortable in collaborative, fast-moving environments
> - Motivated by **real-world impact**, especially where software supports people and operations
>
> ## TASKS
> 1) **Answer the prompt below** in **1–2 strong paragraphs** (unless the question requires a different length).
> 2) Use **2–3 specific proof points** from my resume (projects/work) to support the fit.
> 3) Explicitly connect:
>    - My technical skills → **reliability, data quality, security, and scalability** in mission-critical environments
>    - My working style → **nimble, hands-on teamwork**
>    - My motivation → **patient-centered impact** without pretending I have clinical expertise
> 4) Keep the tone **clear, grounded, confident**. No buzzwords. No corporate marketing voice.
>
> ## DO NOT
> - Invent pharma/clinical experience I don’t have
> - Overstate what I built or claim outcomes I didn’t measure
> - Use generic lines like “passionate about helping people” without a concrete reason tied to my work
>
> ## OUTPUT FORMAT
> - Final answer (1–2 paragraphs)
> - Then 3 bullets: **“What I emphasized and why”**
